Snapchat Planet Order: Complete Guide to the Snapchat Solar System

Social media platforms constantly introduce new features to keep users engaged, and Snapchat is known for creating unique interactive tools. One of the most interesting features available to premium users is the Snapchat Solar System. This feature visually represents your best friends list using planets that orbit around you.

The concept of the Snapchat Planet Order is simple yet fascinating. It ranks your closest friends using the same order as the real solar system. The friend you interact with the most becomes Mercury, while others follow in planetary order depending on your interaction level.

This creative system helps users understand their Snapchat friendships in a visual and entertaining way. Instead of simply viewing a list of friends, you can see your relationships displayed as a solar system where interaction determines the position of each planet.

What Is the Snapchat Solar System?

The Snapchat Solar System is a feature available in Snapchat Plus that turns your best friends list into a solar system model. In this system, you represent the Sun, and your closest friends orbit around you as planets.

The closer a planet is to the sun, the stronger your interaction with that friend. The further away the planet is, the less frequently you interact with that person on Snapchat.

Snapchat Planet Order Explained

The Snapchat planet order follows the same sequence as the real solar system. Each planet represents a different ranking among your best friends.

  • Mercury
  • Venus
  • Earth
  • Mars
  • Jupiter
  • Saturn
  • Uranus
  • Neptune

These eight planets represent your top eight friends on Snapchat based on interaction level.

Mercury – Your #1 Best Friend

Mercury is the closest planet to the sun and represents your number one best friend on Snapchat. This is the person you interact with the most through snaps, chats, and streaks.

If someone appears as Mercury in your Snapchat Solar System, it means your communication with them is stronger than with anyone else on the platform.

Venus – Second Best Friend

Venus represents your second closest friend on Snapchat. You communicate with this person frequently, but slightly less than your number one best friend.

This position still indicates a strong friendship with regular snaps and conversations.

Earth – Third Best Friend

Earth represents the third position in the Snapchat planet order. This friend still interacts with you often but with slightly lower activity compared to the top two friends.

In the Snapchat Solar System, Earth is often shown with a moon, making it visually distinctive.

Mars – Fourth Best Friend

Mars represents the fourth closest friend in your Snapchat ranking. This indicates consistent communication but with less interaction than the top three friends.

This friend might regularly reply to your stories or exchange snaps with you occasionally.

Jupiter – Fifth Best Friend

Jupiter represents the fifth friend in your Snapchat best friends list. Even though this friend may not be among your closest contacts, you still interact with them often enough to keep them in your top eight.

This ranking often includes friends you talk to occasionally or share snaps with regularly.

Saturn – Sixth Best Friend

Saturn represents the sixth position in the Snapchat planet order. This indicates moderate interaction with a friend.

You may communicate with this person from time to time but not as frequently as with your closest friends.

Uranus – Seventh Best Friend

Uranus represents the seventh friend in your Snapchat Solar System. Interaction with this friend usually happens occasionally.

Although they are not among your closest contacts, they still remain in your top friends list.

Neptune – Eighth Best Friend

Neptune is the last planet in the Snapchat planet order and represents the eighth friend in your best friends ranking.

This friend still interacts with you on Snapchat but less frequently compared to others in the solar system.

How Snapchat Determines Planet Ranking

Snapchat determines the planet ranking based on your overall interaction with friends on the platform.

  • Number of snaps sent and received
  • Chat messages
  • Snap streak activity
  • Story replies and reactions
  • Overall engagement frequency

The more interaction you have with a friend, the closer their planet appears to the sun.

Final Thoughts

The Snapchat planet order is a creative feature that turns your best friends list into an interactive solar system. Each planet represents a level of interaction, helping users understand their friendships in a fun and visual way.

From Mercury as your closest friend to Neptune as your eighth friend, the Snapchat Solar System provides an entertaining way to see how your Snapchat relationships evolve over time.
